I began skateboarding when I was 4 years old. It started as a family tradition but now I mostly skateboard with my friends. Skateboarding is a dangerous sport. The only ways I've learned is through failing whether that be through falling once, twice, thrice, or more. This same principle applies for my interest in computer science. Aspects of this study, such as learning and implementing a new algorithm, debugging code, or wishing you could physically tear documentation in half (despite it being digital), are all a part of picking yourself back up, and educating yourself. It's true that humans tend to focus on the tough things in life. Not that we should make education terrible, but it should be a process of wrestling with the knowledge presented to us -

for when we hit our lowest point, we are open to the greatest change.
